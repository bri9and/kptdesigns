These 50 briefs are the build spec. One per concept, in slug order. Each pairs a concrete hero composition, real trade-flavored copy, named sections, motion behavior, material vocabulary, trade-fit reasoning, and explicit differentiators against nearest neighbors. No placeholder copy.

### v23-fieldnotes

#### Hero
Stacked composition on a kraft-paper field. A pocket-sized notebook fills the right two-thirds, dot grid showing through, with a rubber-stamped section head and a graphite-pencil headline written in a steady hand. Left third: ink-stamped logo, hand-numbered date, a clipped chalk-flag.

- Headline: "Stake-and-flag walks. Spec'd to plan. Mulch on Friday."
- Subhead: "A landscape designer's pocket book — the notes that survive a drizzle and end up as installs."
- Primary CTA: "Walk a property"
- Secondary CTA: "See the field journal"

#### Sections
1. **Today's Walk** — Horizontal scroll of dated entries with rubber-stamped headers and pencil margin notes. Hover reveals the chalk-flag color used at that stake.
2. **Specs (softscape / hardscape)** — Two-column ledger; softscape entries in nursery-tag italic Latin, hardscape in stamped block caps. Toggle switches column emphasis.
3. **Maintenance Calendar** — Grid of months as numbered Field Notes pages; each page lists the edge-and-blow rhythm.
4. **Footer** — Stamped "drafted in the truck cab" credit line, USDA Hardiness Zone, copyright as a date-stamp.

#### Motion
Page enters as a notebook flip from the right edge with a 350ms damped settle. Scroll advances pages with a half-second damp; corner curl peeks on hover. Pencil-stroke underline draws left-to-right under any link on focus.

#### Materials
Kraft cover stock, dot-grid bond, rubber-stamp ink, graphite pencil, chalk-flag nylon. Tactile and working — the artifact has been in a back pocket.

#### Why this concept fits landscaping
Designers genuinely carry Field Notes Expedition for stake-and-flag walks because regular paper turns to mush. The site is the working artifact, not a decoration of one.

#### Differentiators (vs nearest neighbors)
Distinct from v29-paper-mache by being notebook-led (single bound object, dot-grid rigor) instead of layered torn-collage. Distinct from v60-letterpress by leaning on graphite/stamp informality, not pressed cotton-paper formality.
