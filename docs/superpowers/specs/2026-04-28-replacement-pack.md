## Section 2 — v58-group-text Chrome Rewrite

**Direction chosen:** **Radio dispatch / fleet-management terminal aesthetic.**

A GC running 8–15 group threads is closer to a dispatcher running channels than to a producer running a set. The thesis (GC dispatch as a working group thread) survives intact; the chrome moves from Apple iMessage to a fleet/dispatch terminal — channel-labelled message log, time-coded entries in monospace, RX/TX indicators, a green-on-charcoal CRT register where consumer messaging chrome used to live. Nothing about iOS — no "9:41," no `#0A84FF`, no SF Pro, no Apple bubble shape.

---

#### v58-group-text  (rewritten)

- **name:** V58 — Group Text
- **emoji:** 📻
- **descriptor:** Fleet-dispatch terminal — channel-labelled crew log on charcoal panel, monospace timecodes, amber RX / green TX indicators, magnetic-strip channel tabs.
- **risk:** Medium Risk
- **appeal:** Bold
- **thesis:** GC dispatch as a working group thread — but rendered as a fleet-dispatch terminal, not an iPhone. The page is a charcoal-faced console with channel tabs (CH-1 FRAMERS, CH-2 MASONRY, CH-3 MEP, CH-4 INSPECT) running across the top; a scrolling crew log fills the middle; entries time-stamp in monospace with the speaker's initials on the left and the photo / voice-memo card on the right. Distinct from v13-conversation (existing AI dialogue) by being multi-party crew chatter, and now distinct from any consumer-messaging look — the visual is industrial dispatch, not chat.
- **palette:** Console Charcoal #1B1E22, RX Amber #E8A03A, TX Green #4FB872
- **type:** Headings: IBM Plex Mono Bold (Google Fonts). Body: IBM Plex Mono Regular. UI labels: IBM Plex Sans Semibold all-caps.

#### Hero
Full-bleed charcoal console panel. Top edge: four channel tabs in IBM Plex Sans all-caps — `CH-1 FRAMERS  CH-2 MASONRY  CH-3 MEP  CH-4 INSPECT` — with a magnetic-strip indicator under the active one in TX green. The middle is a scrolling dispatch log: each entry is a single row, monospace timestamp on the left (`07:14`), two-letter speaker code (`MK > CH-1`), a one-line dispatch in IBM Plex Mono, and a small media badge on the right (`📷 photo`, `🎙 0:09`, `⊘ ack`). RX-amber LEDs blink along the right rail when a sub is "keying up." The wordmark sits in the upper-left corner as a stenciled console label.

- Headline: "Framers in at 7. Masonry at 9. Dry-in by Friday."
- Subhead: "General contracting where the channel IS the schedule — we run the dispatch, you read the log, the job ships clean."
- Primary CTA: `[ KEY UP ]` — a chunky black console button with an amber LED.
- Secondary CTA: `[ MONITOR ]` — same affordance, dimmer LED.

#### Sections
1. **Today's Log** — A scrolling dispatch log on CH-1. Each entry: timestamp, speaker, line. Hovering an entry pulls a copper hairline under it and fades the rest of the log to 35%.
2. **Photo Drops** — Inline expanding jobsite photo cards on a sub-rail; a small monospace caption (`MK / 0729 / SLAB DRYIN /  62F`) below each. Tap-to-expand within the row, no modal.
3. **Memo Tape** — Voice-memo entries rendered as a horizontal tape strip — green TX bars run while a memo is "playing," and the transcript types out under the bar in monospace as the bars run. Pause stops the bars; reduced-motion shows the transcript whole.
4. **Channel Switch** — A tabbed view: clicking CH-2 MASONRY swaps the log without page reload; the magnetic strip slides to the new tab with a 200ms damp; channel-color hue (cool teal on CH-2, copper on CH-3) recolors the timestamp column to keep channels distinguishable.
5. **Footer** — Console-stamp credit line, AIA membership, contractor's license, FCC-style call-sign block (`KPT-DISPATCH / OP MK / LIC GC-114882`).

#### Motion
Page enters with a console power-on flicker (3-frame ramp of the green TX rail, then the amber RX rail). Each new log entry slides up from the bottom with a 1-frame typewriter feel; the timestamp counts in monospace. Channel-switch slides the magnetic strip and recolors the timestamp column. RX-amber LEDs pulse softly when a memo is "live." All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`: under reduce, the log presents fully populated, LEDs hold steady, no slide-in, no transcript typing.

#### Materials
Powder-coated charcoal panel, brushed aluminum bezel, amber and green LED indicators, stenciled call-sign labels, IBM Plex Mono on the log, magnetic channel tabs, hairline copper rules. Industrial dispatch — a Motorola fleet-radio terminal cross-bred with a job-trailer schedule board.

#### Why this fits general-contractors
The most-used GC tool is the group text — a working GC juggles 8–15 threads, and whoever answers fastest wins repeat work (per trade-cues). Rendering that as a dispatch terminal honors how GCs already think of themselves: not as producers, but as the operator running the channels. Channel-labelled chatter is closer to the lived experience than consumer-messaging chrome ever was.

#### Differentiators
Distinct from v13-conversation (existing AI dialogue) by being multi-party crew chatter on labelled channels, not single-thread Q&A. Distinct from v32-permit-board (coroplast jobsite signage) by being the live dispatch, not the static placard. Distinct from v40-lightboard (live circuit schematic) and the original cut iMessage chrome — no consumer-OS skin, no Apple system blue, no SF Pro, no `9:41`. The IBM Plex Mono / amber-and-green / dispatch-console combination doesn't appear anywhere else in v1–v72.

- **motion:** see brief above. Console power-on, log slide-in, channel-switch recolor, LED pulse — all under `prefers-reduced-motion` gates.
- **tradeFit:** general-contractors
- **tradeShowcase:** general-contractors
- **category:** Conversational

### v34-bubble-level

#### Hero
A milled-aluminum torpedo-level body fills the right two-thirds of the screen at 2× life-size, three glass vials running horizontally across it (plumb / level / 45°). The level vial holds the bubble dead-center on first paint; the brass end-caps glint. Left third: a wordmark stenciled into the aluminum, project counter, dispatch number.

- Headline: "Plumb. Level. Tested."
- Subhead: "Service plumbing for the houses where nothing's supposed to leave plumb — DWV rough-in, repipe, and the inspection that didn't punch."
- Primary CTA: "Call a plumber"
- Secondary CTA: "Book a level-check"

#### Sections
1. **Run the Vials** — Three sections under three vials: PLUMB (vertical work), LEVEL (horizontal runs), 45° (offsets and traps). Each section's content lives inside the vial; the bubble centers as you scroll into anchor.
2. **Closet Flange Gallery** — Macro photographs of trim-out details — closet flanges shimmed to plumb, hub-and-spigot transitions wiped clean — captioned in monospace.
3. **The Inspection** — A single-card reveal of a stamped inspection slip, "PASS — NO PUNCH," date and inspector's initials.
4. **Footer** — Stamped milled-aluminum credit line, master-plumber license, UPC-section reference.

#### Motion
Bubble glides to center as the active section enters viewport. Hovering an end-cap pops the dimension callout. A wipe-and-shimmer runs across the aluminum body on first paint. Reduced-motion locks the bubble center and presents all sections fully.

#### Materials
Milled aluminum body, amber glass vials, brass end-caps, stencil-stamped serial. Instrument-grade — the artifact lives in a leather pouch in a service truck.

#### Why this concept fits plumbing
Trade-cues call out "torpedo level across a closet flange, shimmed with a cedar shake" — the level is THE plumber's verification instrument. The site IS that instrument, the bubble IS the trust signal. Earned, not metaphorical.

#### Differentiators (vs nearest neighbors)
Distinct from v31-pipe-stack by being instrument-led (a tool with vials and a bubble) rather than system-led (an isometric DWV drawing). Distinct from v56-pipe-cam by being a static instrument, not a fish-eye camera POV. Distinct from v57-soldering by being measurement, not craft macro.

### v51-broadsheet

#### Hero
Full-bleed tabloid front page on pulp newsprint. A 96pt cap-height banner head ("PIPE BURST — CALLS ANSWERED") fills the top fold; a single-color halftone of a snake-cable pull dominates the right; a classifieds column in 6pt agate runs the right rail. Register marks bleed at the gutter; a small `VOL. XII NO. 4 / LATE EDITION` masthead sits under the banner.

- Headline: "Pipe Burst — Calls Answered."
- Subhead: "Service plumbing, in print: callouts, cleanouts, and the 2 a.m. stack that didn't wait."
- Primary CTA: "Read the late edition"
- Secondary CTA: "Place a callout"

#### Sections
1. **Front Page** — Banner story, halftone photo, byline in italic, dropcap on the lede. The hero headline doubles as a navigation anchor.
2. **Local Beat** — Two-column body copy in Tiempos Text on pulp. Ledes are real callout stories ("RAINSTORM / 03:14 — main line cleared at 04:08"). Each story footnoted with the response time.
3. **Classifieds** — A real classifieds column in 6pt agate: services for hire, after-hours rates, the master-plumber license. The classifieds are the pricing card.
4. **Editorial Cartoon** — A single-panel halftone illustration in Press Cherry — a clogged trap arm with a wry caption. Distinct from a logo, distinct from a mascot.
5. **Footer** — Press credit line, masthead repeat, UPC chapter cite, master-plumber license number.

#### Motion
Banner head slams in with a 1-frame ink-impression squish. Halftone images develop dot-by-dot on viewport-enter (skipped under reduced-motion). Hover on a classified line darkens its rule. Scroll turns the page edge-curl from bottom-right corner.

#### Materials
Pulp newsprint, single-color (Press Cherry) plus banner black, 6pt agate classifieds, halftone dots at 65 lpi, gutter register marks. Working-class print register — loud and blunt and honest.

#### Why this concept fits plumbing (showcase)
Service plumbing is dispatch-and-response work — front-page material every shift. The tabloid grammar (loud headlines, time-coded stories, classifieds-as-pricing) honors how the trade actually moves. And as agnostic, the broadsheet skin can carry any callout-driven trade.

#### Differentiators (vs nearest neighbors)
Distinct from v3-editorial (existing — polished offset magazine spread) by being pulp newsprint with halftones and 96pt banners, not a Vogue-grade layout. Distinct from v59-isotype by being narrative-led, not pictogram-led. Distinct from v60-letterpress by being industrial press on pulp, not Crane's Lettra cotton bite. Distinct from v53-color-block by being typographic-loud, not Swiss-restrained.

### v54-sandwich-board

#### Hero
A sidewalk A-frame chalkboard fills the left two-thirds of the screen, propped on two terracotta sandbags at the foot. The slate has been written, erased, and re-written — smudge-ghosts of last week's lettering haunt the surface beneath this week's "DESIGN / INSTALL / MAINTAIN" in bistro-script chalk. A small printed credit-line — Söhne Buch — runs along the wood frame at the bottom: "EST. 2014 / DESIGN-BUILD LANDSCAPE."

- Headline: "Design / Install / Maintain"
- Subhead: "Design-build landscape for owners who want the same crew through the whole season — stake, pour, plant, mow, and the mulch ring done right."
- Primary CTA: "Walk the property"
- Secondary CTA: "See this season's installs"

#### Sections
1. **The Three Lines** — Three boards stacked vertically on first scroll; each is a service category written in chalk: DESIGN (hand-drawn icon of a stake-and-flag), INSTALL (icon of a paver and a shovel), MAINTAIN (icon of a stand-on mower). Hover redraws the chalk fresh.
2. **This Season's Specials** — A bistro-style "TODAY:" board: spring cleanup, mulch refresh, paver re-leveling, with chalk-priced lines and a swipe-and-rewrite when the mouse leaves.
3. **The Walk** — A horizontal strip of project boards — each property as an A-frame chalkboard caption, dated, hand-lettered. Distinct from v28-herbarium (specimen sheets) by being storefront/sidewalk, not catalog/scholarly.
4. **Footer** — Wood-frame credit line, USDA Hardiness Zone (Zone 6b default, or geolocated), magnetic-strip phone, Söhne Buch printed contact card stuck to the corner with a thumbtack.

#### Motion
Chalk type draws on with a slight grit-and-skip stroke (~140ms per letter). Hovering an item dusts the board faintly and rewrites a price next to it. Smudge-ghosts of erased copy fade in under the active line. The A-frame leans 1° in the wind cycle (paused under reduced-motion). Scroll wipes one section's chalk and writes the next.

#### Materials
Slate-painted ply with a chalk-rough texture overlay, bistro-script and Caveat chalk-type, terracotta sandbag at the foot, oak A-frame, thumbtack-stuck card. Hospitality-grade hand-lettering — the trade's storefront when there is no storefront.

#### Why this concept fits landscaping (showcase)
Trade-cues call out the chalkboard sandwich-board as a typographic association for landscaping ("Chalkboard 'design / install / maintain' sandwich-board"). The site IS that board at full size. As an agnostic skin, the sandwich-board carries any service-on-a-route trade — but landscaping owns the cue.

#### Differentiators (vs nearest neighbors)
Distinct from v28-herbarium (catalog/specimen sheets — flat, scholarly) by being storefront/sidewalk hand-lettering. Distinct from v37-zonemap (USDA full-bleed gradient) by being a single tactile board, not a national surface. Distinct from v61-greenhouse (dimensional living glass structure) by being a static painted surface in chalk. Distinct from the chalk-on-tarpaper / chalk-on-plywood concepts (v44-snap-line, v30-saw-line) by being slate sidewalk-board, not jobsite/structural — different chalk altogether.
