# Trade Cues — 50-Mockup Catalog Reference

**2026-04-28 · Trade Researcher.** One page per trade, pulled from practitioner culture — keep concepts native, not costume-y.

---

## Tree Service

### 5 visual cues
- Bar-oil drips on bark and chip-truck running boards
- Husqvarna orange / Stihl gray-and-orange on saws and chaps
- Saddle clutter: prusiks, ascenders, flipline on a side D
- Stump-grinder wheel mottled gray-green after maple
- Bandit or Vermeer chipper feed wheel in late-day dust haze

### 3 typographic associations
- Hand-painted truck-door lettering with drop shadow, "& SON" stacked
- ANSI Z133 cover — black-on-yellow caution-stripe header, condensed gothic
- Aluminum tree-tag stamps: numbered, debossed, slightly off-axis

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- TIP (tie-in point)
- crotch reduction
- redirect
- drop zone
- spar pole

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Cartoon evergreen silhouette logo
- "Branch out" / "go out on a limb" puns
- Lumberjack-with-axe mascot — that's logging, not arboriculture

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Arborists obsess over knot fluency like trad climbers — "rope-runner pro vs. ZigZag" fills 90 minutes of a 7am tailgate.

## Landscaping

### 5 visual cues
- Faded cobalt-and-yellow Stihl blower harness
- Mulch trail behind a stand-on mower; chalk-flag stakes pre-set
- Belgard / Techo-Bloc paver sample boards leaned on a tailgate
- Soil-stained kneepads, a Lufkin red folding rule
- Weed-barrier roll and half-empty Preen on the trailer deck

### 3 typographic associations
- Nursery-tag botanical Latin in italic serif (Acer rubrum 'October Glory')
- Chalkboard "design / install / maintain" sandwich-board
- USDA Hardiness Zone map — sans-serif numerics on banded gradients

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- hardscape vs. softscape
- spec'd to plan
- mulch ring
- edge-and-blow
- lift the canopy

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Grass-blade-shaped letter in a wordmark
- Literal green-thumb thumbprint
- Rainbow-of-flowers stock-photo hero

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Designers carry Field Notes Expedition for stake-and-flag walks — regular paper turns to mush in drizzle.

## Roofing

### 5 visual cues
- Chalk-blue snap-line across fresh underlayment
- Coil-nailer hanging by its hose from a ridge bracket
- Shingle bundles — GAF gray, OC pink, CertainTeed black — at the ridge
- Pipe-boot flashings in graduated sizes lined up on a tarp
- Magnetic-sweeper trail of bent nails like iron filings

### 3 typographic associations
- Warranty card: serif legalese, bold-caps "LIFETIME LIMITED"
- Stenciled deck markings ("4/12 — N SLOPE")
- Hard-hat sticker layering: union locals, manufacturer certs, flag

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- tear-off vs. overlay
- ice-and-water
- step flashing
- valley metal
- 6-nail pattern

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- House silhouette with a literal red roof
- Cartoon raindrops with frowny faces
- Ladder-leaning-on-house-with-sun-rays hero

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Crew leads ID pipe-boot brand by glove-feel — Oatey, Perma-Boot, and Lifetime Tool differ in neoprene durometer enough that a thumb-press names the maker.

## Paving

### 5 visual cues
- Olive-drab Asphalt Zipper or LeeBoy 8500
- Steam off fresh 305°F mat warping the truck behind
- Tack-coat black on a curb, crisp screed line
- MSA V-Gard hi-vis-orange against dust-gray milled surface
- Thermometer probe in the windrow, three temperatures circled on a notepad

### 3 typographic associations
- DOT spec-book — Highway Gothic / FHWA Series E condensed
- "FRESH OIL — DO NOT DRIVE" on yellow corrugated plastic
- License-plate condensed sans on truck doors

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- mat temperature
- joint density
- screed extension
- tack coat
- mill-and-fill

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Cartoon road with curving white center line as logo flourish
- "Smooth as glass" / "paving the way" puns
- Literal steamroller mascot

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Crews track NOAA hourly dewpoint like farmers — a 12mph crosswind kills the compaction window faster than rain.

## Plumbing

### 5 visual cues
- Bucket of fittings sorted by size, copper-to-PEX transitions on top
- Cobalt Ridgid wrench handle, yellow Ridgid 300 power drive
- PVC primer purple-staining a can rim on flattened cardboard
- Inspection mirror and flashlight in a back pocket, headlamp on the cap
- Torpedo level across a closet flange, shimmed with a cedar shake

### 3 typographic associations
- UPC / IPC code-book — small-caps section headers in tight serif
- Rubber-stamp blueprint titleblock with handwritten revision letters
- Sharpie'd labels on copper stub-outs ("HOT — KIT", "COLD — LAV")

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- rough-in
- pipe dope
- trap arm
- cleanout
- DWV (drain-waste-vent)

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Crossed-wrenches cartoon logo
- Plumber's-crack jokes
- Anthropomorphic blue water-droplet mascot with eyes

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Service plumbers date a house within 5 years from one look under a sink — "that's a '78 stack" is said unironically.

## Electricians

### 5 visual cues
- Klein high-leverage side-cutters, yellow-and-red dipped grip
- Roll of 12/2 Romex slung on a forearm, ends fanned into a panel
- Chalky-orange Greenlee 555 conduit bender
- Panel directory in tight all-caps draftsman's hand
- Wirenuts color-sorted in a tray — Ideal Wing-Nuts, not the off-brand

### 3 typographic associations
- NEC code-book — Lippincott serif of NFPA 70 article headings
- Brady or Panduit heat-shrink labels, crisp black-on-white sans
- ANSI Z535 "Danger / High Voltage" placards, orange/black

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- home run
- pigtail
- AFCI / GFCI
- pull box
- ampacity derate

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Lightning-bolt-through-the-O wordmark
- Cartoon frizzy-hair shock-victim character
- "Light up your life" sun-rays-over-a-house hero

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Commercial techs read building age by the 60Hz hum of the gear room — a ten-year tech names the decade before reading the nameplate.

## Carpenters

### 5 visual cues
- Stabila 78" plate level in a corner, vials lit by work-light
- Flat carpenter's pencil behind the ear, sharpened with a utility knife
- Festool Domino on a folding MFT, green dust-extractor hose in a helix
- Shop-vac hose around a sawhorse leg; a third sawhorse holds coffee
- Worn Occidental framer's bag, tongue darkened from the hammer ball

### 3 typographic associations
- Architectural hand-lettering — Leroy or Ames-guide all-caps
- Story-pole Roman-numerals burned into a 1x4
- Stencil type on plywood cutoffs ("KEEP — 11¼")

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- crown the joist
- proud / shy (1/16 proud)
- toenail
- scribe to the wall
- rack the wall

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Crossed-hammers logo
- Sawdust-flecked-beard hero shot
- "Measure twice, cut once" in a script font

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- Finish carpenters argue pencils with religious intensity — Pentel P205 vs. flat carpenter's vs. Blackwing 602 — and the right answer changes by task.

## General Contractors

### 5 visual cues
- Dashboard pile: hard-hat, daily-log clipboard, laminated trade-license, two phones, half-eaten burrito
- Blueprint roll in a cardboard tube on the passenger floor, end-cap missing
- Procore or Buildertrend open on a Toughbook in the trailer
- Trailer white-board schedule, magnetic strips per sub, color-coded
- Trailer-door boot-scrape mat, mud the color of local soil

### 3 typographic associations
- AIA contract typography — tight serif on A-series forms
- Permit placard — bold city-issued sans, blanks filled by hand
- Coroplast site sign: project big, GC medium, subs small

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- punch list
- RFI (request for information)
- dry-in
- change order
- critical path

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Hard-hat-and-blueprints model pointing at something
- Skyline-with-crane silhouette hero
- "We build dreams" in a humanist serif

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- The most-used GC tool is the group text — a working GC juggles 8–15 threads, and whoever answers fastest wins repeat work.

## Handyman

### 5 visual cues
- Pegboard with tool outlines drawn in black Sharpie
- Vintage turquoise Channellock or red Klein 5-in-1 screwdriver
- Harbor Freight magnetic tray, mismatched screws sorted by length
- Open Milwaukee Packout stack in a Transit Connect cargo bay
- Carbonless yellow-copy invoice book, half-filled

### 3 typographic associations
- Magnetic truck signage — Highway Gothic on white, hand-applied phone
- Fridge-magnet business cards — die-cut, glossy, owner's photo
- Hardware-aisle signage — Ace red, chunky condensed sans

### 5 vocabulary anchors
- honey-do
- patch and paint
- caulk and call it
- turnover (rental turnover)
- one-call-does-it-all (used self-deprecatingly)

### 3 visual clichés to avoid
- Thumbs-up smiling-handyman stock photo
- "No job too small" in friendly Comic-Sans-adjacent faces
- Toolbelt-and-baseball-cap mascot

### 1 surprising-but-true association
- The economic engine is the apartment-turnover gig with property managers — recurring, predictable, paid in 14 days. The PM list is the moat; residential one-offs are loss leaders.
