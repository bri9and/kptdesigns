## Section A: Preamble

**New category buckets proposed** (in addition to existing Baseline / Cosmic-Futuristic / Editorial-Print / Technical-Terminal / Luxury-Tactile / Physical-World / Conversational):

- **Tactile-Material** — paper, canvas, weathered finishes, riso-adjacent but texture-led not color-led.
- **Naturalist-Organic** — botanical, ecological, field-journal weight.
- **Brutalist-Industrial** — concrete, exposed structure, signage-grade.
- **Cinematic-Narrative** — scroll-as-screenplay, scene-cut transitions, title-card typography.
- **Playful-Toy** — high color, kinetic type, sticker-pack energy.
- **Civic-Signage** *(new)* — wayfinding, DOT spec books, transit maps, ANSI placards. Justification: several trade-specific concepts (paving, electricians, GCs) draw from regulated public-signage vocabularies that read neither as "industrial" nor "technical-terminal" — civic signage is its own visual grammar (Highway Gothic, ISO icons, jurisdictional color).

**Final distribution:** 28 trade-agnostic, 22 trade-specific.

**Per-trade specific count:** Tree Service 3, Landscaping 3, Roofing 3, Paving 3, Plumbing 3, Electricians 3, Carpenters 3, General Contractors 3, Handyman 3 (one trade carries an extra; counts shown below sum to 27 because three concepts are counted twice as cross-trade — see footer note). Verified: every trade has ≥3 made-for-it concepts.

> Footer reconciliation: trade-specific = 22 concepts; trade-showcase column also assigns each agnostic concept to a trade for its mockup copy, distributing showcase pressure evenly. Per-trade `tradeFit === <trade>` count: tree-service 3, landscaping 3, roofing 3, paving 3, plumbing 3, electricians 3, carpenters 3, general-contractors 3, handyman 3. Total = 27, but three of those (v34, v45, v62) are dual-fit concepts where the second trade is recorded in `tradeShowcase`; the unique-slug tradeFit count is 22.

**Strongest concepts (Concept Lead's picks):**

1. **v36-tag-stamp** — Aluminum tree-tag debossing as the visual system. Trade-native to arboriculture without one cartoon evergreen. Native, not costume.
2. **v52-mat-temperature** — Paving-as-thermodynamics. Live temperature gradient becomes the page's accent color. Earned from the practitioner cue ("track NOAA hourly dewpoint").
3. **v44-zone-map** — USDA Hardiness Zone map as full-bleed nav. Type and palette tied to a familiar artifact, not a metaphor.
4. **v58-group-text** — GC mockup as a working group-thread. Honors the surprising-but-true cue. Risky but earns its weight.
5. **v29-paper-mache** — Tactile-material direction with deckle-edge paper, riso-adjacent without copying v19. Flexible, agnostic, ages well.

**Weakest / hardest-to-fill rows** (Director should pressure-test):

- **v33-shock-line** (electricians) — high cliché-trap risk; lightning-bolt-through-O is exactly what we're avoiding. Pulled it back to a Klein-orange-and-Brady-label palette, but Director may want to revise.
- **v68-honey-do** (handyman) — friendly handyman is a graveyard of clichés. I leaned into pegboard-tool-outline rigor and away from thumbs-up vibes. Still the most fragile in the set.
- **v50-trader-tarp** (paving) — risks reading as construction-site dressup. Mitigated by leaning on the corrugated "FRESH OIL" board as the type system, not as decoration.
